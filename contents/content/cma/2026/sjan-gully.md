Robert and I hiked up Pine Creek in the Absoroka Mountains to reach
some waterfalls, Green Gully (WI4) and Blue Gully (WI3).

We crossed Pine Creek on logs and made a sometimes-tough bushwhack up
to the base of Green Gully. It should normally be in "fat" conditions but
it was quite thin! Robert led the 60 meter long pitch. It was great fun to
follow. The upper quarter of the climb had several rock moves and thin ice
over rock in a chimney. Extremely interesting! We abseiled down...

Then we went over to Blue Gully. I led up this, placing the first screw
20 meters up, then sometimes travelling a good ways before the next screw
because of thin ice over rock. Once again, very interesting though
definitely easier than Green Gully. Still, it was a full 60, or maybe 55
meter pitch. I belayed Robert up from the anchor on the right at the top.
We abseiled down and made the awkward descend on shifting boulders
under snow, suffering crampon-ball-up on our micro-spikes.

On the way back we drove up Trail Creek to return to Bozeman just for fun.
Gorgeous country.
