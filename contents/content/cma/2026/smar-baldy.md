I wore my ice boots and carried snowshoes and spikes, along with an
ice ax for this hike up Baldy Mountain in the Snake River Range, near the
town of Alpine.

Wow, the wind was cold at the start! My feet were warm in ice boots, but
I wore a light fleece jacket all day, and spent the first 30 minutes
climbing steeply and fighting frozen fingers!

Finally, I was warm enough. I made it more than 2000 feet up before snow
became heavier. Finally, on the summit of Little Baldy Mountain I put
snowshoes on, and gee, the work really began here. I made tracks up the
ridge, avoiding cornices. Finally I stood on top and looked north to the
Grand Teton. What a great view in a cold place!

Coming down was much faster. Only 6 miles round trip, but it felt like a
full day with 4600 feet elevation gain/loss.
